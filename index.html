NEPQ "Brush-off" Cheatsheet

Never "handle" an objection. Ask questions to get behind the concern and help the prospect overcome it themselves.[p. 56]

Part 1: The Brush-off Pivots

"I'm just looking for pricing."
Core NEPQ Principle: Never give a proposal or quote without first diagnosing the problem. A "Trusted Authority" must diagnose before they prescribe.
  • "That's not a problem. Just so I can put together the *best* information for you, what exactly are you looking for?" This often leads into the "Accelerated NEPQ" flow.
  • "I'd be open to putting together a proposal for you, but I'm not quite sure I could even help you yet... ...could I ask a few questions about your situation... to be able to put something together for you that might be useful?
    *(Then ask your first Situation Question):* 'For example, what are you using now... [for your fleet]?'"
  • "I can get you that. What is it that you are hoping to see from the quote?"
  • "I will get to work on putting together a quote for you... Now, let's assume... we get you the quote and we were able to meet your needs... What do you see as the next step?"[p. 45]

"I don't have time right now / Call me back."
Core NEPQ Principle: A "Trusted Authority's" time is also valuable. Do not be "needy". Establish professional posture and set a *firm* next step.[p. 46] Use these *first*. If they still resist, you can *try* the "Accelerated NEPQ" flow.
  • "That's not a problem. I can give you my number and you'll have to call me back if you'd like?"
  • "My number is [Your Number]. What's your timeframe on getting back to me just to see if I would be available for you?"
  • "If you have your calendar handy, I could pull out mine to book a specific time with you just so you don't have to chase me down and vice versa... would that be appropriate?"
  • If they resist scheduling: "I understand you're busy. Would you have just 60 seconds if I asked one quick question to see if it even makes sense for us to talk later?" (Then try the Accelerated NEPQ flow).

"This isn't a priority right now."
Core NEPQ Principle: This simply means they do not feel enough *pain* or urgency. Your job is to find out if there is *any* problem to solve.[p. 41]
  • "I understand. I was just curious, what prompted you to look into changing this... [by requesting info] in the first place?"[p. 22]
  • "That's fair. It sounds like things are going fairly well for you... is there anything you would change about... [how you manage your fleet] if you could?"[p. 12]
  • "I see. How important is it for you to solve this problem... [of not knowing where your vehicles are]? Why is that [not a priority] now though?"[p. 14]
  • "I understand. Have you given up trying to deal with this problem?"[p. 28]

"We're already using [Competitor]."
Core NEPQ Principle: This is a "Vendor Disruption" opportunity. Do *not* pitch your features. Your only goal is to find the *gap*—the small dissatisfaction they have.[p. 19]
  • "That's great. To me, it sounds like things are going fairly well for you with [Competitor]... is there anything you would change about... [their service] if you could?" *(If they answer):* "Why change that though?"
  • "If you could change one thing about who you use now, what would that be?"
  • "Can I ask in what ways could your vendor do better for you than what they are doing now though?"
  • "Back when you chose to work with that company... what was your selection criteria...? In what ways has that (criteria) changed as you look at your needs today though?"[p. 20]

"Can you send me some references?"
Core NEPQ Principle: This is often a way to get rid of you. You must qualify their intent and secure a next step.[p. 43]
  • "That's not a problem, I'm curious what would you like to ask them when you call?" (This uncovers their real concern.)[p. 44]
  • "So that I can send the right people to you, what specifically would you like to discuss with them?"[p. 44]
  • "I can reach out to them... Now, let's pretend for a moment that the clients you talk to say good things... where do you think we should go from there?" (This is the key qualifying/commitment question.)[p. 44]

"We don't have the budget for this."
This means you haven't built enough value by finding their *pain*. The problem you've found isn't big enough (yet).[p. 31]
  • "And I can appreciate that money might be an issue... from what you told me, how do you think you can resolve that so that you can..." [Repeat back the solution they said they wanted][p. 32]
  • "You had mentioned that the most important thing to you is cost, can I ask how that compares to you actually getting results and being able to solve this problem though?"[p. 25]
  • "Tell me, if you *did* have the funding... is this something that would work for you? ...Why do you feel it would though?"[p. 32]
  • "If you don't decide to address this problem now, how much will it cost you this year in lost... [profit/fuel/etc]?"[p. 30]

Part 2: The Framework

Your NEPQ Mindset
Your goal is to help them find a problem they didn't know they had, or one they didn't know they could solve.
  • You are a "Trusted Authority" and a "Problem Finder," like a doctor.
  • You are NOT a "product pusher."
  • You are here to ask questions and *diagnose*, not to pitch, present, or persuade.
  • People buy on **emotion** (pain) and justify with logic. Your job is to find the *emotional* reason for change.
  • DON'T talk about your products (GPS, dash cams). DO ask questions about their problems (fuel costs, driver behavior, lost time).
  • Your TONE is more important than your words. Be neutral, calm, and curious (like a doctor), not "salesy" or "enthusiastic."
  • Use a skeptical or confused tone when asking "why" questions to pull out more emotion.
  • Without pain, there is no sale.

Inbound Call Framework (One-Call Close)
Use this framework AFTER you successfully pivot past an initial brush-off. Acknowledge their interest, then dive into diagnosis.
Initial Opening (First 15 Seconds)
  • You: "Hi, is this [Lead Name]?" (Wait for confirmation)
  • You: "Hi [Lead Name], this is [Your Name] calling from Verizon Connect on a recorded line. You had put in a request online just a bit ago about our [GPS Tracking / Dash Cams]... Is now still okay for a quick chat?" This references *their* action.
  • ➡️ If they say "No, I'm busy" or "Just send pricing" -> Use the **Brush-off Pivots** in Column 1.
  • ➡️ If they say "Yes" or anything neutral -> Proceed to Stage 1 below.
1. Connecting / Situation (~0-1 Minute After Opening)
  • You: "Great. So, you were looking into [GPS Tracking / Dash Cams]... **What are you doing now...** for that?"[p. 11] Goal: Establish their current state quickly.
2. Problem Awareness (~1-5 Minutes)
  • You: "Gotcha. And **what do you like or... what *don't* you like** about [calling drivers / using Airtags / current system]?"[p. 12] Focus on the "don't like." Use probing questions below to dig.
  • You (Probing): "When you say [it's a hassle / the reporting isn't great], **how do you mean exactly?**"[p. 22]
  • You (Probing): "**What's causing that issue?**"[p. 22]
  • You (Probing - The KEY Pain Question): "**Has that had an impact on you? In what way?**"[p. 12] Listen for $$$, time wasted, frustration, customer complaints, risk.
3. Solution Awareness (~1-2 Minutes)
  • You: "Okay, I see. So, **what have you done about changing this, if anything?**"[p. 13] Find out if they've tried other things and failed.
  • You: "And ideally, **what would you do about it if you could, though?**"[p. 13] Let them describe their perfect solution.
4. Consequence (~1 Minute)
  • You: "That makes sense. **What if you don't do anything about this problem** and [repeat the pain - fuel costs stay high / you keep wasting time / customer complaints continue]?"[p. 14] Build urgency by focusing on the negative future.
5. Qualifying (~1 Minute)
  • You: "So, **how important is it for you to solve this problem?**"[p. 14]
  • You (Crucial follow-up): "**Why is that important to you *now* though?**"[p. 14] This confirms their urgency and emotional driver.
6. Uncover Decision Process (~1 Minute)
  • You: "Before I show you how we might be able to help... **Can you walk me through the criteria you use** when picking a company for something like this?"[p. 24]
  • You: "And besides yourself, **who else might be involved** in making a final decision?"[p. 31] Don't skip this - avoids "I need to talk to my boss" later.
7. Transition (~30 Seconds)
  • You: "Okay, based on what you told me about [problem 1] and how it's making you feel [emotion/impact], and how you're looking for [their ideal solution]... what we do might actually work for you... [Briefly mention the core benefit related to their pain]... **Does that feel as if it might be what you're looking for?**"[p. 15] Do NOT pitch features here. Just bridge their pain to the result.
  • You (Crucial follow-up): "**Why do you feel like it is though?**"[p. 15] Makes them sell themselves on why it's a fit.
8. "Presenting" / Confirming (~2-5+ Minutes)
  • Only NOW do you briefly show/explain *only* the parts of Verizon Connect that solve their *specific* stated problems. Use "Checking for Agreement" questions constantly.[p. 39] You: "...so you can see the live map here... **Do you see how that could help you** avoid those customer calls?"[p. 39] You: "...and this report shows idling time... **How do you see that helping you** the most with fuel costs?"[p. 40]
9. Committing (~1-2 Minutes)
  • You: "So, **do you feel like this could be the answer for you?**"[p. 15]
  • You (Crucial follow-up): "**Why though?**"[p. 15] Again, makes them sell themselves.
  • You (The Close - UPDATED): "Well, I don't have anything else to go over with you, it looks like we covered what you're looking for. Really, the next step is pretty simple. I just need to confirm the best shipping address for the equipment and your preferred billing method—we can do ACH or credit card. Once I have that, I'll send over a quick DocuSign agreement. After you sign that, you can just send over the VIN numbers for the vehicles, and we'll get everything scheduled for you." (Adjust details as needed)[p. 15 - adapted]
  • You (Final Question): "**Would that be appropriate, or how would you like to proceed from here?**"[p. 16]

How to Dig Deeper (Probing)
Use these when you get short, logical, or vague answers during the "Problem Awareness" stage. These questions find the *emotion* and *pain*.[p. 22]
  • "Tell me more..."[p. 25]
  • "How do you mean exactly?"
  • "Can you be more specific? Give me an example..."
  • "What's causing this issue?"
  • "Why so important now?"
  • "Has that had an impact on you? In what way?"[p. 12]
  • "How did you feel about that?"[p. 26]
  • "What would this do for you personally?"

How to Uncover the Decision Process
Use these *before* the transition (Stage 6 in the Framework) to understand *how* they buy, so you don't get stuck later.[p. 24]
  • "Can you walk me through the criteria you use when picking a company to solve these issues...?"
  • "Let's suppose you are looking at several different companies who all meet your criteria, including price. How would you then decide what to do?"[p. 25]
  • "In addition to you, who else recognizes that this is a major problem in the company?"[p. 30]
  • "Who else besides you would be involved in approving the budget to solve this?"[p. 31]

How to "Present" (by NOT Presenting)
During Stage 8 of the Framework, your "presentation" is just proving you can solve their stated problems. Ask these "Checking for Agreement" questions *constantly*.[p. 39]
  • "Does that make sense?"
  • "Are we on the same page?"
  • "What are your thoughts on that?"
  • "Are you with me on this?"
  • "Do you see how that works?"
  • "Do you see how that could help you?"
  • "How do you see that helping you the most?"[p. 40]

Accelerated NEPQ (Time Short / Price Focus)
Use this when the prospect says "I don't have time," "Call me back," or "Just send pricing." Your pivots lead here to rapidly find pain.

Scenario A: "I just call my drivers."

Minute 0:00 - The Call Begins

You: "Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with Verizon Connect, how's it going?"

Prospect: "Hey, I'm good, but I'm actually really busy right now. Can you just email me the pricing?"

You (Pivot): "That's not a problem. Just so I send the *right* info, what are you using now to track your vehicles?"

Prospect: "I just call my drivers when I need them."

You (Problem): "Okay. And what do you like or... what *don't* you like about doing it that way?"

Finding the PAIN

Prospect: "Well, it's a hassle. Sometimes they don't answer, and I have to call multiple times. Plus, customers keep calling *me* asking where the truck is."

You (Dig Deeper): "Has that had an impact on you? In what way? Having to call them multiple times and deal with customer calls?"

Prospect: "Yeah, it wastes a ton of my time! And last week, a customer got angry because I couldn't give them an ETA, said we seemed unprofessional."

You (Consequence): "What if you don't do anything about this and you keep wasting that time and customers keep getting frustrated?"

Building Value & Urgency

Prospect: "I can't keep doing that. I need to look professional and stop wasting time."

You (Qualify): "So, how important is it for you to solve this problem... *now*?"

Prospect: "Pretty important."

You (Transition): "Okay. So, if I could show you a way to see exactly where all your drivers are on a map, in real-time, *without* having to call them, so you save time and can give customers exact ETAs... Does that feel as if it might be what you're looking for?"

Prospect: "Yeah, that sounds much better."

You (Commit): "Do you feel like this could be the answer for you? ...Really, the next step is..."

Scenario B: "I use Airtags / another basic tracker."

Minute 0:00 - The Call Begins

You: "Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with Verizon Connect, thanks for taking my call."

Prospect: "Yeah, no problem. Look, I'm kinda swamped, I was really just looking for pricing on your GPS."

You (Pivot): "I can get you that. Just so I send the right info, what are you using now to track?"

Prospect: "We just use Airtags in the trucks."

You (Problem): "Gotcha. And what do you like or... what *don't* you like about using Airtags?"

Finding the PAIN

Prospect: "They're okay for location, but they don't tell me anything else. I don't know how fast my guys are driving, or if they're braking hard, or how long they idle."

You (Dig Deeper): "What's causing you to want to know those things, like speed or idling?"

Prospect: "My fuel bill is insane! And my insurance just went up because one of my guys got into a fender bender."

You (Consequence): "What if you don't do anything about this and your fuel costs stay high and insurance keeps going up?"

Building Value & Urgency

Prospect: "I can't afford that. I need to get those costs under control."

You (Qualify): "So, how important is it for you to solve this problem... *now*?"

Prospect: "Very important."

You (Transition): "Okay. So, if I could show you a system that not only tracks location but also gives you reports on speed, idling, and harsh braking, helping you cut fuel costs and potentially lower insurance... Does that feel as if it might be what you're looking for?"

Prospect: "Yes, definitely."

You (Commit): "Do you feel like this could be the answer for you? ...Really, the next step is..."
